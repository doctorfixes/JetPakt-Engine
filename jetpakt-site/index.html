How we stay safe

Designed to protect you, not embarrass you.

Advisory work goes sideways when software takes shortcuts. Ours is built around four non-negotiable rules.

Rule 01

Defamation-safe quoting

Every finding references a verbatim quote that is already public on Google or Yelp. We never name individual staff. We never invent a claim. We never repeat an unverified illness allegation as fact.

Rule 02

Legal-review flags

Wage, service-fee, billing, and food-safety language is automatically flagged for your attorney before any response template is sent. HB25 1090 (effective January 1, 2026) is tracked for every Colorado operator.

Rule 03

Drafts-only delivery

Every response lands as a draft in your inbox. No auto-posting. No auto-email. No per-row exceptions. You are the last decision every time.

Rule 04

Auditable workflow

Every Pulse run is snapshotted, diffed, and logged. You can reconstruct what we knew, when we knew it, and which template was suggested, down to the verbatim source quote.

Frequently asked

Questions we get from owners.

Do you post responses on our behalf?

No. Everything we produce is a draft sent to the owner's inbox. You review, edit if needed, and post. We never auto-post to Google, Yelp, or any public channel.

Will you name our employees?

No. Our audits and drafts never name individual staff, even when reviewers do. Staff first names only appear inside a verbatim quoted review where we need to preserve the source, and even then we will flag it for your attention.

How is this different from a reputation management service?

Most reputation tools sell automation: auto-requests, auto-responses, mass review generation. JetPakt Solutions is the opposite: a consultant-grade audit layer with human approval gates. We are built for owners who want clean, defensible, auditable reputation work, not volume tactics.

Do you work with chains or franchises?

At this stage we focus on independent, owner-operated Denver metro businesses. Chains and franchise units are outside our current scope, since they typically have corporate reputation teams with different priorities.

What does HB25 1090 have to do with my business?

Colorado's HB25 1090 (Junk Fees Law) took effect January 1, 2026. It changes how service fees and automatic charges must be disclosed. We track every mention of service-fee, surcharge, or automatic-gratuity language in your public reviews and flag items that could attract regulatory attention or a private-action suit. Your attorney makes the legal call. We just make sure nothing is missed.

Can I cancel a Pulse subscription?

Anytime. No contracts, no cancellation fees. Cancel and we stop the next cycle. You keep every PDF and draft already delivered.
